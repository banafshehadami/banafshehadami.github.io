About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of South Florida. My research develops machine learning and computer vision methods for secure biometrics, healthcare AI, and trustworthy intelligent systems.

I am broadly interested in inverse problem formulations and their applications in biomedical and biometric sensing, including remote physiological signal reconstruction from video. I also work on generative models—from diffusion and GAN-based methods to large language models (LLMs)— for representation learning, signal recovery, and presentation attack detection.

Current projects include anti-spoofing for contactless fingerprint systems, rPPG and rECG modeling, and robust deep learning under real-world conditions. I serve as a reviewer for conferences and journals in signal processing, biometrics, and machine learning.
